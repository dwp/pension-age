When you can get your State Pension

The earliest you can get your State Pension is 6 May 2019, when you're 66 years and 3 months.

What happens next

You'll soon get a letter telling you how to claim your State Pension. When you get this letter you can either:

  • make a claim to get your State Pension
  • delay getting your State Pension - you may get more State Pension when you come to claim it

You can also check your State Pension to find out how much you could get.

Other age-related support

When you reach State Pension age, you may also be eligible for:

  • Winter Fuel Payments - you'll get these automatically if you're getting State Pension
  • a bus pass - you can apply to your local council for free travel
  • Pension Credit - if you're on a low income, you may be able to get this
