What you’ll get if you qualify

How much you get depends on your circumstances during the qualifying week. The qualifying week for winter 2019 to 2020 is 16 to 22 September 2019.

Any money you get will not affect your other benefits.

If you were born 23 September 1939 to 5 April 1954, you’ll get:

  • £200 if you live alone (or none of the people you live with qualify)
  • £100 if you live with someone who also qualifies
  • £100 if you live in a care home and do not get certain benefits

If you were born on or before 22 September 1939, you’ll get:

  • £300 if you live alone (or none of the people you live with qualify)
  • £200 if you live with someone under 80 who also qualifies
  • £150 if you live with someone 80 or over who also qualifies
  • £150 if you live in a care home and do not get certain benefits

If you qualify and get certain benefits

Your payment may be different if you or your partner get one of these benefits:

  • Pension Credit
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Income Support

If you were born 23 September 1939 to 5 April 1954, you’ll get:

  • £200 if you get one of the benefits and live alone (or none of the people you live with qualify)
  • £200 if you...
  • no Winter Fuel Payment if you live in a care home and get one of the benefits

If you were born on or before 22 September 1939, you’ll get:

  • £300 if you get one of the benefits and live alone (or none of the people you live with qualify)
  • £300 if you live with someone under 80 who also qualifies
  • no Winter Fuel Payment if you live in a care home and get one of the benefits

How and when you are paid

You’ll get a letter in November telling you how much, an estimate of when and details of which account it’ll be paid into.

Most payments are made automatically between November and December.
