What you’ll get if you qualify

How much you get depends on your eligibility and your circumstances during the qualifying week. The qualifying week for winter 2020 to 2021 is from 21 to 27 September 2020.

You can check to see if you're eligible for a Winter Fuel Payment online.

Any money you get is tax-free and will not affect your other benefits.

If you get certain benefits

Your payment may be different if you get:

  • Pension Credit
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Income Support

Born 21 September 1940 to 5 October 1954

You'll get:

  • £200 if you live alone (or none of the people you live with qualify)
  • £100 if you live with someone who also qualifies
  • £100 if you live in a care home and do not get certain benefits
  • £100 if you move into a care home after 29 June 2020 and get certain benefits
  • no Winter Fuel Payment if you live in a care home for the whole time from 29 June to 27 September 2020 and get certain benefits

Born on or before 22 September 1939

You'll get:

  • £300 if you live alone (or none of the people you live with qualify)
  • £200 if you live with someone under 80 who also qualifies
  • £150 if you live with someone 80 or over who also qualifies
  • £150 if you live in a care home and do not get certain benefits
  • £150 if you move into a care home after 29 June 2020 and get certain benefits
  • no Winter Fuel Payment if you live in a care home for the whole time from 29 June to 27 September 2020 and get certain benefits

How and when you are paid

You’ll get a letter in October or November to say how much you'll get. The letter will also give an estimate of when it'll be paid and give details of the account it’ll be paid into.

Most payments are made automatically in November or December.

Contact the Winter Fuel Payment Centre if you do not get your payment by 15 January 2021.
